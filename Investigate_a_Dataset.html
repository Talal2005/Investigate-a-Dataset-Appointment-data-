Conclusions¶

There is a strong inverse relationship between the gender of the patient and his attendance at the appointment or not, as the value of the correlation reached to -0.85

The average age of those who attended their appointment was slightly higher than the average age of those who missed their appointment,reaching 37 years compared to 34 years to who missed.

The age group from one to twenty years is the most committed age group to attend the appointment, and it is also the most age group than the rest of the groups that did not attend the appointment.

The hospital needs to raise the quality of work efficiency and speed of completion in order to reduce the number of patients who missed the appointment.

The data that provides the timing of booking the appointment and the appointment data itself seem illogical, if this data is provided correctly, an analysis of other factors can be provided in the reasons for attending the appointment, such as the efficiency of work in the hospital itself.

No missing or duplicate data, with the data linked with a unified patient identifier correctly.

The age data had an incorrect entry. It was clear that it was the result of a simple error. It was modified by adding the average so that the patient's data could be used.
